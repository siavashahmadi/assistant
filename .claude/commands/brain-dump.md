Sia is doing a brain dump. Help him get everything out of his head and into an organized system.

## Step 1: Load Context
Read:
- `memory/daily-log.md` — what's already tracked
- `memory/projects.md` — active projects for categorization

## Step 2: Capture
Ask Sia to dump everything on his mind. Tell him:
- Don't filter, don't organize, just type
- Incomplete thoughts are fine
- Mix of work, personal, ideas, worries — all welcome
- Type "done" when finished

## Step 3: Categorize
Sort everything into:

**Do** — Actionable tasks with a clear next step
- Break vague items into concrete first actions
- Estimate size: small (< 30 min), medium (1-2 hrs), large (half day+)

**Decide** — Needs a decision before action
- Frame the actual decision clearly
- List the options if obvious

**Explore** — Ideas, curiosities, things to research
- Note why it's interesting

**Let Go** — Worries or tasks that aren't actually actionable right now
- Acknowledge it, explain why it can wait

## Step 4: Prioritize the "Do" list
Pick the top 3 items for today/this week. Use these criteria:
- What's blocking other things?
- What has a deadline?
- What would feel the best to get done?

## Step 5: File Into Memory

After presenting, organize items into the appropriate memory files:
- Action items and priorities → append to `memory/daily-log.md` under today's entry
- Project-related items → update `memory/projects.md`
- Job search items → update `memory/private/job-search.md`
- New ideas or learnings → `memory/learnings.md`

Ask Sia before writing to confirm the organization looks right.

## Step 6: Next Step
Ask if Sia wants to start working on the #1 item right now.

## Rules
- Present the organized output cleanly with headers and bullets.
- The value is in the organizing — don't skip categorization.
- Always file results into memory so nothing gets lost.
