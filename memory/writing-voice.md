# Sia's Writing Voice — Professional Communications

_Derived from sent emails, March 2026. Use this to match tone in cover letters, follow-ups, and application responses._

## Core Personality
Professional yet personable. Not corporate. The vibe is: "I'm good at what I do, I'm easy to work with, and I don't take myself too seriously." Think confident engineer who happens to be a likable human.

## Greeting Rules
- **"Hey [Name],"** — default with recruiters he's spoken to before, or casual contexts
- **"Hi [Name],"** — default for first contact or slightly more formal situations
- **Never** "Dear," "To whom it may concern," or "I hope this email finds you well"

## Opener Patterns (real examples)
- "Thanks for the update! Excited and happy to be moving forward."
- "It's a pleasure to speak with you again!"
- "Thanks for your email, and apologies for the delayed response—I just saw it."
- "Seems like all the business is happening in NYC now!"
- "Oh no worries haha."
- "Thank you for reaching out. Thank you for the transparency as well."

## When Pitching Himself
Gets technical and specific but stays conversational. Uses em dashes, concrete numbers, and keeps it moving. No filler.
- "At Joulea I owned the full IoT data pipeline end-to-end — from sensor ingestion through RabbitMQ, normalization with AI (LLM-powered to handle vendor inconsistencies), fault detection, and dual storage routing to RDS and S3. It processes ~10M data points/day across 200+ commercial buildings."
- "The last 3 years I've been at a startup working on things from 0 to 1. We have grown from a company of 3 to 17."

## Sign-offs
- **"Best, Siavash Ahmadi"** — standard
- **"Best, Siavash"** — with people he has rapport with
- **"Thanks, Siavash Ahmadi"** — when asking for something

## Rejection Handling (graceful, not desperate)
- "Although I'm disappointed that this time didn't work out, I thoroughly enjoyed learning about [company]'s approach..."
- "I fully understand the decision and respect the outcome."
- Always leaves the door open: "I hope we can stay in touch" / "I hope our paths may cross again"

## What He DOESN'T Do
- No corporate jargon or buzzwords ("synergy," "leverage," "passionate about innovation")
- Never lengthy — most emails are 2-5 sentences
- Doesn't grovel or over-thank
- Doesn't overuse exclamation marks — uses them sparingly and genuinely
- Doesn't use "I hope this email finds you well" or "Per my last email"
- Doesn't hedge excessively — says what he means

## The Balance
- **Serious mode:** Technical specificity, concrete numbers, clear about what he built and how. Used when pitching skills or responding to detailed questions.
- **Light mode:** "Oh no worries haha," "Hello?" as a follow-up, casual openers like "Seems like all the business is happening in NYC now!" Used when rapport exists.
- **Default mode:** Warm, direct, brief. Professional but you can tell there's a real person on the other end.

## Cover Letter Tone Guide
- Open with something specific about the company — not generic admiration
- Get to the technical match fast — 2-3 concrete bullet points with numbers
- Close genuine — what actually interests him about the work, not "I would be honored"
- Keep it under 250 words. Sia's emails are short. His cover letters should be too.
