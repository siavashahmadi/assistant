"Tell me about yourself."
▼
I'm a software engineer with about 5 years of experience, mostly building full-stack products and AI features at B2B startups.

I started at T-Mobile where I worked on large-scale enterprise systems — Angular apps serving 20 million users, Java microservices handling thousands of transactions a day. Great for learning scale and discipline.

Then I moved to Joulea, an IoT and energy SaaS startup, where I've had a lot more ownership. I architected the full platform — React, Flask, AWS — and built some really interesting AI features, including an LLM and RAG chatbot that hit 85% accuracy and a generative AI labeling system that sped up a manual data process by 67%. I also built the data pipeline that handles over 10 million data points a day and reduced infrastructure costs by 57% after a cold storage migration.

I'm now looking for my next step — somewhere early-stage where I can have real impact, keep building AI-native products, and grow fast.
Delivery tips
Keep it under 90 seconds. This is the opener, not the whole story.
Pause after each company. Don't rush through it.
End with what you're looking for — let it flow into why their company.
Career Arc
"Walk me through your background / resume."
▼
Sure. I studied Computer Science at Georgia State — Honors College, graduated in 2020. Right out of school I joined T-Mobile as a software engineer. It was a great environment to learn how large engineering organizations operate — I was building Angular components on their prepaid platform, writing Java microservices, dealing with PCI compliance, OAuth. Solid foundation.

After about two years there I wanted to be somewhere smaller where I could own more. So I joined Joulea, which is a startup building IoT and energy analytics for commercial buildings. I've been there since mid-2023 and I've worn a lot of hats — full-stack product work, data pipeline design, and more recently a big push into AI features. I built a RAG-based support chatbot from the ground up, and a generative AI workflow that automated our most painful manual data process.

Now I'm looking for a role where I can go deeper on the AI side and work somewhere with more momentum.
Delivery tips
Don't just list jobs — explain the logic of why you made each move.
"I wanted to own more" is a clean, honest reason. Say it confidently.
Land on where you are NOW and why you're looking. That's what they care about.
Sensitive
"Why are you leaving your current role?"
▼
Joulea has been a great place to grow — I've had real ownership and built things I'm genuinely proud of. But it's a small team and at this point I've hit a ceiling in terms of growth and exposure. I want to be somewhere with more momentum, where I'm working alongside more senior engineers and on a product that's scaling faster. Specifically the AI space has my full attention right now, and I want to go all in on that.
Delivery tips
Never trash your current company. Stay positive and forward-looking.
"Ceiling on growth" is honest and non-controversial.
Pivot fast to what you're excited about — not what you're running from.
Vision
"What are you looking for in your next role?"
▼
A few things. First, real ownership — I do my best work when I can take a problem from spec to production without a lot of handholding. Second, AI-native work. Not just integrating an API, but actually thinking about models, retrieval strategies, agent architectures. That's where I've been spending my energy and I want to go deeper. And third, somewhere with momentum — a team that's growing, a product people actually use and care about. I'm not looking for a chill job. I want to be around people who are building seriously.
Delivery tips
Say three things max. More than that sounds unfocused.
"I'm not looking for a chill job" is a strong, honest line. Own it.
After this, pivot: "From what I've heard about [company], it sounds like X…"
